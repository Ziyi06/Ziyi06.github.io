I have a passion for combining different geochemical data sets to study Earth's crustal evolution. I recently obtained my PhD degree from the Australian National University (ANU) in January 2023. During my time at ANU, I focused on using global zircon geochronology and isotope geochemistry data to examine various crustal processes in Earth's history, including sediment melting to form S-type granites, high mountain formation through continent-continent collisions, and continental crust growth. As a postdoctoral scholar at Monash University, I am keen to deepen my understanding of the factors driving high mountain formation and develop a model to estimate ancient mountain heights using numerical methods.

If you're interested in my research, don't hesitate to reach out. I'm open to collaborations.
